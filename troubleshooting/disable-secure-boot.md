[](#how-to-disable-secure-boot)How to Disable Secure BootTo disable Secure Boot on your system, follow these steps:

Restart your computer and enter the BIOS/UEFI settings.

The key to access BIOS/UEFI often varies (e.g., F2, Delete, Esc, or F10).

Navigate to the Security or Boot tab using the arrow keys.

Locate the Secure Boot option.

Change the setting to Disabled.

Save your changes and exit the BIOS/UEFI settings.

Your computer will restart with Secure Boot disabled.

Note: The instructions might slightly vary depending on the manufacturer. Always consult your motherboard manual or support documentation for specific guidance.
